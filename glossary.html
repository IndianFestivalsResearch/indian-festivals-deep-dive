Regional Name Notes

  • Diwali ≠ Kali Puja ≠ Deepavali: In Bengal, Diwali night is Kali Puja — a different goddess. Lakshmi Puja occurs on Kojagari Purnima (October full moon), a separate date. "Diwali" as a pan-Indian term erases this difference.
  • Eid has Malayalam names: In Kerala, Eid-ul-Fitr is Cheriya Perunnal (Small Festival) and Eid-ul-Adha is Valiya Perunnal (Big Festival). These are uniquely Malayali terms not used elsewhere in India.
  • Holi is Dol in the East: Bengal, Odisha, and Assam call it Dol Jatra/Dol Purnima — more musical and devotional than the colour-riot of North India.
  • Pongal/Sankranti has the most names: Pongal (Tamil), Sankranti (Kannada/Telugu), Uttarayan (Gujarati), Makar Sankranti (Hindi belt), Lohri (Punjabi), Magh Bihu (Assamese), Poush Sankranti (Bengali) — all the same solar date, but different festivals.
  • Gurpurab vs Guru Nanak Jayanti: Sikhs say Gurpurab or Prakash Utsav. "Guru Nanak Jayanti" is a Sanskritised term used more by non-Sikhs and in official contexts.
