Indian Festivals Deep Dive

A research-based, culturally respectful guide to the festivals of the Indian subcontinent — their regional lives, historical origins, diaspora afterlives, and the people who keep them alive.

Diwali

Deepavali · Tihar · Bandi Chhor Divas

The festival of lights celebrated across India with lamps, fireworks, sweets, and new beginnings. Multiple origin stories across Hindu, Jain, and Sikh traditions.

HinduJainSikhPan-Indian

Holi

Dol Jatra · Phagwah · Rangapanchami · Yaosang

Spring festival of colours, bonfires, and exuberant joy. Ancient roots as a sun-charm and fertility rite. Celebrated from Braj to the Caribbean.

HinduCultural

Durga Puja · Navaratri · Dussehra

Durga Pujo · Dasara · Garba · Bomma Golu

Nine nights of the Goddess, culminating in victory. UNESCO-recognised Durga Puja in Kolkata; garba in Gujarat; Mysore Dasara; Ramleela across the North.

HinduUNESCO 2021

Eid-ul-Fitr · Eid-ul-Adha

Meethi Eid · Bakri Eid · Perunnal

Islamic festivals celebrated in India for over 1,400 years. End-of-Ramadan feasting and the Feast of Sacrifice. Distinctive regional cuisines and traditions.

Muslim

Ganesh Chaturthi

Vinayaka Chavithi · Ganeshotsav · Pillayar Chaturthi

Elephant-headed god's birthday. Transformed from a household ritual into a massive public festival by Tilak in 1893 as anticolonial mobilisation. Dhol-tasha, pandals, and immersion.

HinduModern origin

Krishna Janmashtami

Gokulashtami · Dahi Handi · Nandotsav

Midnight celebration of Krishna's birth. Human pyramids for Dahi Handi in Maharashtra; Rasleela in Braj; fasting and bhajans worldwide through ISKCON.

Hindu

Maha Shivaratri

Shivratri · Herath (Kashmir) · Mandi Shivaratri

The Great Night of Shiva. All-night vigil, fasting, and abhishek at the twelve Jyotirlingas. Kashmiri Pandits' Herath is a distinctive tradition.

Hindu

Raksha Bandhan

Rakhi · Avani Avittam · Janai Purnima

Sibling-bond festival. The protective thread was historically tied between many relationship types — the brother-sister framing is a modern crystallisation.

HinduSikhSocial

Makar Sankranti · Pongal · Lohri

Uttarayan · Magh Bihu · Poush Sankranti

The only solar-calendar festival. Kite-flying in Gujarat, bonfires in Punjab, sweet rice overflowing in Tamil Nadu, pithey in Bengal. Same date — entirely different festivals.

HinduHarvest

Onam

Thiruvonam

Kerala's harvest festival celebrated by all religions. The legendary King Mahabali returns. 26+ item banana-leaf feast (sadya), snake-boat races, and flower carpets.

HinduMuslimChristianState festival

Chhath Puja

Surya Shashti · Dala Chhath

Rigorous four-day sun-worship festival from Bihar/Eastern UP. Women-led, waterless fasting, standing in rivers offering arghya to the setting and rising sun. Carried by migration to metros and diaspora.

HinduWomen-led

Baisakhi · Vaisakhi

Vaisakhi · Baisakhi

Sikhism's most important festival: the 1699 founding of the Khalsa by Guru Gobind Singh. Also a Punjabi harvest festival and solar New Year across India.

SikhHinduHarvest

Gurpurab

Guru Nanak Jayanti · Prakash Utsav

Guru Nanak's birth anniversary. Akhand Path, Nagar Kirtans, and langar at the Golden Temple and worldwide. The Kartarpur Corridor now enables cross-border pilgrimage.

Sikh

Mahavir Jayanti

Mahavir Janma Kalyanak

The most important Jain festival. Birth of the 24th Tirthankara, Mahavira. Processions, abhishek, charity, and a 10-day birth celebration tradition recorded in the Kalpasutra.

Jain

Buddha Purnima

Vesak · Buddha Jayanti · Saga Dawa

The day of the Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and death. Celebrated at Bodh Gaya, Sarnath, and across Buddhist communities — from Himalayan monasteries to Ambedkarite rallies.

Buddhist

Christmas in India

Christmas · Bada Din

One of the oldest continuous Christmas traditions in the world. Kerala's Syrian Christians trace their practice to the 4th century CE. Distinct Goan, Northeastern, and metropolitan traditions.

ChristianSecular

Eid-e-Milad-un-Nabi

Barawafat · Mawlid

Prophet Muhammad's birth anniversary. Processions, naat, qawwali, and illuminated streets. Internal diversity: some reform movements consider it an innovation; others celebrate elaborately.

Muslim

Nowruz · Jamshedi Navroz

Pateti · Parsi New Year

Parsi Zoroastrian New Year. Celebrated by India's tiny but culturally significant Parsi community. Fire temples, special feasts, and charitable giving. Two different calendar dates observed.

Zoroastrian

Hornbill Festival

Festival of Festivals

Created in 2000, this 10-day Nagaland festival brings together all 16+ Naga tribes. A modern meta-festival reclaiming indigenous heritage in a Christian-majority state. Chili-eating contests included.

IndigenousModern

Sarhul & Karma

Sarhul · Karma Puja

Adivasi spring and autumn festivals of Jharkhand and neighbouring states. Sal flower worship, the marriage of Earth and Sun, and the demand for Sarna religious recognition in the census.

AdivasiHarvest

Bonalu & Bathukamma

Bonalu · Bathukamma

Telangana's state festivals. Bonalu: Shakta folk festival with decorated pots and tranced dancers. Bathukamma: women's floral cone-stack celebration. Both elevated post-2014 statehood.

HinduState festival
