Sambat Jalana: The Festival That Predates Holi

Blog Post #1 — Research Notes

In 1896, the British ethnographer W. Crooke documented something remarkable in the hills of Mirzapur. The Dravidian tribes there did not celebrate Holi — but on the exact same date, they burned a stake in a ceremony called Sambat Jalana, "the burning of the old year." This practice may represent a pre-Hindu festival layer that predates the Holi we know by centuries — perhaps millennia. Read more →

Coming Soon: When the Bells Fly to Rome — Easter Traditions Indians Don't Know About

Blog Post #3 — Regional Discovery

Goan children are told that church bells "fly to Rome" during the Easter Triduum. Kerala Christians eat unleavened bread descended from Jewish Passover traditions. Northeast choirs rehearse for months. Indian Easter is far richer than the Anglo-American version most Indians encounter in malls and movies.
