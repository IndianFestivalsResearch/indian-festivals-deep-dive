Easter in India

The Resurrection: Lent · Holy Week · Pascha
✝️ Christian (all denominations: Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant) 📍 Major in: Kerala, Goa, Northeast, Tamil Nadu, Mangalore, Mumbai 📅 March–April (first Sunday after the Paschal full moon) ⏳ Celebrated in Kerala since at least the 4th century CE

Theologically Central, Culturally Indian

For Christians, Easter is the most significant festival — more important than Christmas. It marks the resurrection of Jesus Christ after his crucifixion, preceded by the 40-day Lenten fast and the intense rituals of Holy Week (Palm Sunday, Maundy Thursday, Good Friday, and Easter Vigil). In India, Easter has been celebrated for as long as Christianity has existed here — which is far longer than most people realise.

India's Syrian Christians (Kerala) trace their origins to St. Thomas the Apostle (traditionally 52 CE). While the earliest documented evidence dates to the 4th century CE, this still makes Indian Easter one of the oldest continuously observed Easter traditions in the world — older than the Christianisation of most of Europe.

Regional Easter Traditions

Region / CommunityEaster Traditions
Kerala — Syrian ChristiansPesaha (Maundy Thursday): unleavened bread (Indri Appam) and a coconut-milk-jaggery drink (Pesaha Paal) blessed at home — a ritual with possible Jewish-Christian origins. Good Friday: church services, strict fasting. Easter Sunday: resurrection mass at dawn, followed by a feast of appam, mutton stew, and rich Kerala-style duck or chicken curry. The 50-day Lenten fast ends with celebration.
Goa — CatholicPortuguese-influenced Holy Week processions. The Santos Passos (Stations of the Cross) parade through Goa's towns with hooded penitents. Good Friday is solemn. Easter Sunday: church bells (silent since Maundy Thursday) ring again, and the feast includes sorpotel (spicy pork), vindaloo, and bebinca (layered dessert). Feni might appear.
Northeast (Nagaland, Mizoram, Meghalaya)In Christian-majority states, Easter is the most important festival of the year. Church services, community feasts, and choir competitions. Traditional tribal foods are served alongside rice beer in some communities. The Baptist and Presbyterian traditions dominate — Easter is more austere than Goan Catholic Easter but deeply felt.
Tamil NaduThe Velankanni basilica (Our Lady of Good Health) draws pilgrims. Tamil Catholic Easter includes midnight mass and the post-Lenten feast. St. Thomas Mount in Chennai is a pilgrimage site tied to the apostle's martyrdom.
Mumbai / MangaloreEast Indian Catholic community (original Mumbai Christians) and Mangalorean Catholics have distinctive Easter traditions. Mangalorean Catholic Easter pork dishes (sorpotel, pork bafat) are legendary.

Fun Facts & Light Mirth

The Kerala Pesaha Appam (unleavened Passover bread) is one of the strongest pieces of evidence for Jewish-Christian origins of the Kerala Syrian Christian community. It's essentially a Christianised version of the Jewish matzah — made from rice flour, urad dal, and coconut, steamed in a cross-embossed vessel. There are only a handful of Christian communities worldwide with a continuous unleavened-bread tradition.
In Goa, church bells are silent from Maundy Thursday to the Easter Vigil — a period called the "Triduum." Children are told that "the bells have flown to Rome." Then on Easter morning, the bells "return" and ring triumphantly. This Portuguese Catholic tradition is now distinctly Goan.
Nagaland is ~90% Christian, making it one of the most Christian-majority regions in Asia outside the Philippines and East Timor. Easter in Kohima (the capital) is a state-wide holiday with streets largely empty except for church traffic. The Baptist church services can run for hours and include full-choir hymns in Naga languages.
